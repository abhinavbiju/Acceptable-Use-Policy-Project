## Overview

This project presents a **corporate Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)** designed to define appropriate and secure use of organizational systems, networks, and data.

The policy is based on best practices from the **SANS Institute Security Policy Templates** and aligns with common governance frameworks used in enterprise environments.

---

## Objective

- Establish clear guidelines for acceptable use of IT resources  
- Reduce cybersecurity risks caused by human behavior  
- Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory standards  
- Protect organizational assets, data, and systems  

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and third-party users  
- All company-owned and personal devices accessing corporate systems  
- All networks, applications, and data owned or managed by the organization  

---

## Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

### 1. Authorized Use

Users are permitted to:
- Access systems only for legitimate business purposes  
- Use company resources in a responsible and ethical manner  
- Follow all security controls and procedures  

---

### 2. Prohibited Activities

Users must NOT:

- Access, store, or distribute unauthorized or illegal content  
- Attempt to bypass security controls or authentication mechanisms  
- Install unauthorized software or applications  
- Share credentials or allow unauthorized access  
- Use company systems for personal financial gain or malicious activity  

---

### 3. Data Protection

- Sensitive data must be handled according to classification policies  
- Users must not transmit confidential data over unsecured channels  
- Encryption must be used where required  
- Data should only be accessed on a need-to-know basis  

---

### 4. Device Security

- Devices must have updated antivirus and security patches  
- Users must lock devices when unattended  
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately  
- Personal devices accessing corporate systems must meet security standards  

---

### 5. Internet & Email Usage

- Email must not be used for phishing, spam, or malicious activity  
- Users should avoid suspicious links and attachments  
- Internet usage should align with business needs  

---

### 6. Monitoring & Privacy

- The organization reserves the right to monitor system usage  
- Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company systems  

---

### 7. Incident Reporting

Users must report:
- Suspected phishing attempts  
- Security incidents or breaches  
- Lost or compromised credentials  

---

### 8. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Disciplinary action  
- Revocation of access  
- Termination of employment  
- Legal consequences (if applicable)  

---

- Prevent unauthorized access  
- Improve security awareness  
- Strengthen overall security posture
